RUBRICS

The HackVerse Hackathon will be governed by a set of general rules, a code of conduct, and specific guidelines for projects in the AI, ML, and Cybersecurity domains.
The product should be in alignment with UN's SDGs

General Rules & Guidelines

1.Eligibility & Registration: Participants must register online in teams of 2 to 4 members. All team members must be from the same college (if specified by the organizers). No changes in team composition are allowed after the registration deadline.
2.Original Work: All projects must be substantially new and developed entirely during the hackathon's specified timeframe. Brainstorming ideas and creating wireframes beforehand is encouraged, but no coding is allowed before the event begins.
3.Use of Existing Resources: Teams may use open-source libraries, frameworks, or publicly available APIs/SDKs. The use of any external resources or pre-trained models must be clearly cited in the project documentation/README file.
4.Project Submission: Submissions must include a functional prototype or proof-of-concept, a link to the code repository (e.g., GitHub), a detailed description, testing instructions, and a short (2-5 minute) demonstration video.
5.Intellectual Property (IP): Participants typically retain ownership of their project's intellectual property. However, organizers and sponsors may be granted a non-exclusive license to use project details and media for promotional or showcase purposes.
6.Judging & Awards: Projects will be evaluated by a panel of expert judges based on criteria such as innovation, technical implementation, functionality, impact, and user experience. The judges' decision is final and binding.
7.Fair Play: Plagiarism, cheating, or any form of unsporting behavior will result in immediate disqualification. Code reviews may be conducted to ensure compliance.
8.Conduct: Participants must adhere to a code of conduct that promotes inclusivity, respect, and professionalism, ensuring a harassment-free environment for everyone.

Domain-Specific Considerations
1.AI/ML Track
a.Ethical AI: Solutions must adhere to ethical principles and guidelines for AI development and deployment, including fairness, transparency, accountability, and non-discrimination.
b.Data Handling: Teams are responsible for ensuring they have the right to use any datasets for their projects and must comply with data protection and privacy laws.
c.Model Usage: Use of pre-trained models is permitted, provided they are properly cited and their licenses allow for hackathon use.

2.Cybersecurity Track
a.Responsible Hacking: Participants may only perform security testing on systems and applications they have created or have explicit permission to test from the owners.
b.No Harm: Projects must not be designed to harm, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to any third-party systems or data. Any tools developed should focus on defensive security or ethical testing methodologies.
c.Compliance: Solutions related to security controls (e.g., access control, multi-factor authentication) should follow industry best practices.
